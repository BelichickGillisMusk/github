About Mobile Testing

What is mobile CARB testing?

Mobile CARB testing brings licensed emissions testing directly to your location. Instead of driving your truck to a testing station, a CARB-certified tester comes to your yard, driveway, or job site with all the required equipment. You get the same official pass/fail documentation on-site.

Are mobile test results valid for DMV registration?

Yes. All tests are performed by CARB Tester ID IF530523 using CARB-approved equipment. Results are valid for California DMV registration and CARB compliance exactly the same as a shop-based test.

Why choose mobile testing instead of going to a shop?

Mobile testing saves you time and money:

  • Zero downtime: Your truck stays at your yard, earning instead of sitting at a shop.
  • No driving: No fuel cost, no wear, no shop appointment wait times.
  • Same-day results: Official documentation the moment the test completes.
  • Free retest: If you fail, we come back at no charge within 150 days.

Do you test at truck stops or on the road?

Yes. We can meet you at any truck stop, rest area, or distribution center along the I-5 and Highway 99 corridors. Call ahead to coordinate timing.

Testing Requirements

Do I need OBD or OVI-J1667 testing?

  • OBD testing ($75): Required for 2013+ heavy-duty diesel trucks over 14,000 lbs GVWR under CARB's Clean Truck Check program.
  • OVI-J1667 testing ($250): Required for pre-2013 trucks or vehicles exempt from OBD testing under the Periodic Smoke Inspection Program (PSIP).

Not sure which you need? Call 916-890-4427 with your vehicle year, make, and GVWR and we'll tell you.

How often do I need CARB testing?

Biennial (every 2 years) for most heavy-duty diesel trucks. Some fleets may have annual requirements depending on CARB compliance programs. Check your DMV registration renewal notice or CARB account for your specific due date.

What is the CARB Clean Truck Check program?

The Clean Truck Check program requires OBD testing for 2013+ heavy-duty diesel trucks. It's part of California's emissions compliance for commercial vehicles. If your truck is registered in California and weighs over 14,000 lbs GVWR, you need biennial OBD testing.

What is OVI-J1667?

OVI-J1667 stands for Opacity Visible Inspection per SAE J1667 standard. It's a smoke/opacity test for pre-2013 heavy-duty diesel trucks. We measure exhaust opacity during a snap-acceleration test to ensure your truck meets CARB's emissions thresholds.

Do I need CARB testing for my motorhome or RV?

If your motorhome is diesel-powered and registered in California, yes. We provide full-service RV testing (OBD for 2013+, OVI-J1667 for pre-2013) at your location. Pricing is $75 for OBD, $250 for OVI-J1667, or $325 for combined testing if required.

Do I need testing if my truck is out of state?

If you're registering a truck in California for the first time (or re-registering after an out-of-state period), you may need CARB testing. Check with the DMV or call us at 916-890-4427 with your VIN and we'll confirm.

Technical & Equipment

What equipment do you use?

We use CARB-approved OBD diagnostic tools and SAE J1667-compliant opacity measurement equipment. All equipment is calibrated and certified to meet California Air Resources Board standards.

What if my truck has fault codes or a check engine light?

You'll likely fail OBD testing if your check engine light is on or active fault codes are present. We recommend clearing codes and repairing the issue before scheduling your test. If you fail, we provide detailed fault code documentation and offer free retest within 150 days after repairs.

What if my truck's battery is disconnected or codes were recently cleared?

Your truck's OBD system needs to complete drive cycles (readiness monitors) after a battery disconnect or code clear. If monitors aren't ready, you may fail. We recommend driving your truck normally for 50–100 miles before testing to ensure all monitors complete.

Do you test gasoline trucks or light-duty vehicles?

No. We specialize in heavy-duty diesel trucks (14,000+ lbs GVWR) under CARB's Clean Truck Check and Periodic Smoke Inspection programs. For gasoline or light-duty smog checks, visit a standard smog station.
