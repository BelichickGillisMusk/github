Status: Final
Owner: NorCal Carb Mobile
Last updated: 2026-05-13

PURPOSE
-------
Create a simple, compliant cold email sequence for fleet, trucking, repair,
construction, tow, delivery, and logistics leads that need California Clean
Truck Check / CARB heavy-duty emissions testing.

The sequence should move a lead from NEW to CONTACTED in the CRM, create a
clear next follow-up date, and make it easy for Bryan to call or email the
highest-intent operators first.


PRIMARY OFFER
-------------
NorCal Carb Mobile provides CARB-certified mobile Clean Truck Check testing at
the customer's yard. The core value proposition:

- No driving trucks to a station.
- Less vehicle downtime.
- On-site OBD, OVI, and smoke opacity testing.
- Fleet-friendly scheduling and compliance tracking.
- Pricing anchor: OBD $75/vehicle, OVI $85/vehicle, smoke opacity $85/vehicle.
- Fleet discount: 10% off for 5+ vehicles.


TARGET LEADS
------------
Use this sequence for businesses in the CRM with one or more of these traits:

- Heavy-duty trucks, diesel vehicles, buses, tow trucks, delivery vehicles, or
  yard vehicles likely over 14,000 lbs GVWR.
- Operations in Sacramento, Stockton, Roseville, Elk Grove, Modesto, East Bay,
  Vallejo, or nearby Northern California service areas.
- Categories such as trucking company, fleet management, diesel repair shop,
  construction company, tow yard, logistics company, waste management,
  landscaping company, school bus service, or delivery service.
- Tier 1 and Tier 2 leads first. Tier 3 leads can remain phone-first or
  low-frequency email only.


COMPLIANCE AND TONE RULES
-------------------------
- Keep emails plain text.
- Do not imply a prior relationship if there is none.
- Do not overstate penalties; use concrete compliance language sparingly.
- Include Bryan's name, business name, phone, and website.
- Make the ask small: reply, call, or book a 15-minute test window.
- Avoid scare tactics. The compliance deadline is a reason to act, not the only
  reason to respond.
- Use a secondary sending domain and authenticated SMTP.
- Stop emailing immediately when a lead replies, opts out, books, or is marked
  LOST.

SEQUENCE CADENCE
----------------
Touch 1: Day 0 - initial value proposition
Touch 2: Day 2 or 3 - short bump with availability
Touch 3: Day 7 - final note with pricing and compliance reminder

CRM status updates:
- Before sending Touch 1: lead status should be NEW.
- After sending any touch: status becomes CONTACTED.
- If reply received: status becomes RESPONDED.
- If testing booked: status becomes SCHEDULED.
- If no reply after Touch 3: keep CONTACTED and schedule a manual follow-up in
  30 days, or mark LOST if the lead is clearly not a fit.

CATEGORY HOOKS
--------------
trucking company:
Clean Truck Check applies to heavy-duty trucks over 14,000 lbs GVWR, and a lot
of operators are still building a repeatable testing process.

fleet management:
Managing compliance across multiple vehicles is mostly a scheduling problem. We
take the testing trip off your team's plate.

diesel repair shop:
Some shops refer mobile testing after repair work so customers can handle the
test without tying up a bay or sending the vehicle elsewhere.

construction company:
Construction fleets lose money when trucks sit. We test at the yard so vehicles
can stay closer to the job schedule.

tow yard:
Many tow operators have heavy-duty vehicles that fall into Clean Truck Check.
Mobile testing keeps those trucks available for calls.

logistics company:
Warehouse and logistics fleets usually care most about uptime. On-site testing
keeps the compliance stop from turning into a dispatch problem.

waste management:
Refuse and hauling fleets are high-usage vehicles, so keeping testing on a
calendar matters. We can come to the yard.

delivery service:
If any delivery vehicles are over the heavy-duty threshold, they may need Clean
Truck Check testing. We help confirm and test on-site.

school bus service:
Routes are time-sensitive, so mobile testing is usually easier than moving
vehicles to a station.

property management:
If maintenance or hauling vehicles cross the heavy-duty threshold, testing can
be handled on-site instead of interrupting property work.


CRM AUTOMATION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------
1. Email preview should show:
   - business name
   - tier / score
   - selected subject
   - final rendered body
   - missing email warning when applicable

2. Send logic should:
   - skip leads without email addresses
   - skip touches already sent
   - require prior touch before sending the next sequence email
   - log place_id, business_name, email_to, sequence_num, subject, sent_date,
     and status

3. CRM follow-up logic should:
   - increment contact_count when a touch is sent
   - set last_contact to today's date
   - schedule next_followup from the cadence
   - avoid follow-ups for PAID or LOST records

4. Manual work queue should prioritize:
   - overdue follow-ups
   - Tier 1 leads
   - leads with phone and website
   - leads with fleet or compliance category hooks


QUALITY CHECKLIST
-----------------
- Every email has one clear ask.
- No email is longer than necessary.
- Pricing appears only in Touch 3.
- The first email sells convenience, not fear.
- The final email references compliance without sounding like a threat.
- All template tokens have safe fallbacks.
- The CRM can recover if an email address is added after import.
- The sequence can be previewed before any real send.
